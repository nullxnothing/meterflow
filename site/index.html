Dashboard

The dashboard is where Meterflow becomes useful.

The payment rail answers how money moves. The dashboard answers what was metered, who paid, what failed, and whether an agent is still inside policy.

Meters
Price routes

Create products out of APIs, MCP tools, model calls, media routes, data feeds, and compute jobs.

  • Route-level pricing
  • Accepted asset and network
  • Owner and recipient settings
  • Active, paused, and test modes
Receipts
Audit usage

Follow the full path from request to quote to payment proof to response status and receipt export.

  • Payment verification state
  • Request and response metadata
  • CSV and webhook exports
  • Failure reasons and retries
Budgets
Limit agents

Give agents controlled payment ability without handing them unlimited access to a wallet.

  • Daily, weekly, and task caps
  • Endpoint allowlists
  • Revocation and kill switches
  • Operator alerts
Integrations
Attach providers

Connect Solana infrastructure, wallet, data, social, and notification providers to Meterflow routes.

  • Provider route catalog
  • Wallet and settlement context
  • Data and social APIs
  • Operator alerts
Settlement

Payments alone are not the product.

Solana and x402-style flows can move money. Meterflow focuses on the control layer around that movement: quote integrity, usage records, revenue attribution, wallet-linked receipts, and agent-safe limits.

01
Meter

The provider defines the product

Every protected route gets a unit, price, recipient, customer policy, and operational state.

02
Policy

The agent stays inside limits

Budgets, allowlists, and revocation rules decide whether an autonomous caller is allowed to spend.

03
Accounting

The dashboard records the truth

Successful and failed requests become searchable receipts, revenue analytics, and exportable logs.

04
Utility

MFLOW powers the control layer

USDC settles usage. MFLOW supports provider reputation, fee discounts, registry ranking, higher policy limits, longer retention, and future bonding.
